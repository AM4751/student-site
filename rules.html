Texas Hold’em – Rules & Strategy

1. Objective

Each player receives two private “hole” cards. Five community cards are then dealt in stages. Your goal is to make the best possible 5-card poker hand using any combination of your hole cards and the community cards, or to bet in a way that causes all other players to fold.

3. Game Flow

  1. Deal: Each player gets 2 hole-cards.
  2. Pre-flop Betting: In turn, players may fold, call, or raise.
  3. Flop: Burn 1; deal 3 community cards; bet again.
  4. Turn: Burn 1; deal 4th community card; bet again.
  5. River: Burn 1; deal 5th community card; final betting round.
  6. Showdown: Reveal hands; best 5-card hand wins pot.

4. Betting Actions

  • Check: Pass when no bet is outstanding.
  • Bet: Wager chips; creates new amount for others to call.
  • Call: Match current highest bet.
  • Raise: Increase the current bet.
  • Fold: Exit the hand, forfeit any claim to the pot.

5. Showdown & Tiebreakers

  • Select the best 5-card combination from your 7 cards (2 + 5).
  • If ranks tie, highest kicker(s) decide.
  • If completely identical, split the pot evenly.

6. Glossary

Blinds
Forced bets by two players to seed the pot.
Burn
Discarding the top card before dealing community cards.
Kicker
An unpaired card used to break ties when main ranks match.

7. Strategy by Table Size

Heads-Up (2 Players)

  • Play aggressively; widen your range.
  • Steal blinds frequently in position.
  • Use position to apply maximum pressure.

Short-Handed (3–6 Players)

  • Tighten slightly: avoid weak hands out of position.
  • Value-bet your strong hands; multi-way pots are rarer.
  • Pick bluff spots carefully—players defend more often.

Full-Ring (7+ Players)

  • Play tight-aggressive: wait for premium hands early.
  • Late position: widen range slightly to steal blinds.
  • Avoid chasing low-odds draws in multi-way pots.
